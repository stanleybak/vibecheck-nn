LP-DUAL LAGRANGIAN DECOMPOSITION WITH SELECTIVE MILP REFINEMENT
=================================================================

Investigation log: an exploration of Lagrangian-decomposition-style spec lower
bound computation for ReLU networks, ending in a one-shot algorithm built on
LP strong duality + per-sub MILP tightening. Benchmark throughout: ACAS Xu
3_3 prop_2, query 0 (the loosest of the four sub-queries). All numbers are
gap LP = lp_ref - best (negative gap means LD bound is *tighter* than LP
triangle).

Reference values for q0:
  zonotope        -224.08
  CROWN           -177.11
  LP triangle     -163.5993   (joint LP relaxation of the spec)
  MILP racing 30s -102.87     (Gurobi solving the joint spec MILP for 30 s,
                               primal feasible at this value, primal-dual
                               gap +0.04)


1. WHAT WE TRIED, IN ORDER
==========================

The starting question was whether Lagrangian-decomposition spec verification
in the style of Bunel et al. could improve on the existing per-layer LP/MILP
tightening pipeline. We worked through a sequence of methods on q0 and
recorded gap and wall time at each step.

1.1 Per-layer subs, baseline structure
--------------------------------------
For each layer k, build a sub that owns:
  - input_shared[i]: a copy of layer k-1's post_relu[i] (or input box for k=0)
  - W_k @ input_shared + b_k = pre_relu_k
  - ReLU constraints: triangle relaxation (LP-inner) or big-M with binaries
    (MILP-inner)
Plus 5 input subs (1-var LPs, one per input dimension) and 1 spec sub (linear
projection from last hidden layer to y, with the qw·y objective). For ACAS Xu
3_3 with 5 inputs and 6 hidden layers, this gives 5 + 6 + 1 = 12 subs.

Coupling constraints (implicit): for each shared neuron, the consumer's
input_shared[i] must equal the producer's post_relu[i]. Lagrangian dual
multiplier ρ on each pair. The Lagrangian objective becomes:
  L(x, ρ) = base_obj(x) + ρ^T (z_P - z_C)
and is set on each sub via:
  consumer side: -ρ * z_C added to that sub's objective
  producer side: +ρ * z_P added to that sub's objective

Dual function:
  q(ρ) = sum over subs of min(sub objective at ρ)
       <= true spec optimum (by weak Lagrangian duality)

1.2 Iterative methods we tried (full table for q0)
--------------------------------------------------
All on the LP-inner version unless noted. Initial cold-start ρ = 0 gives
q(0) = -230.49 (the trivial bound from independent sub mins at zero coupling).

  method                                              gap LP    wall    notes
  -------------------------------------------------   --------  ------  -----
  cold-start ρ=0                                      +66.89    0.05s   q(0)
  SGD (lr 1e-5 -> 1e-8) 1000 iters                    +0.15     2.1s
  SGD 2000 iters                                      +0.09     4.3s
  Adam best (lr=5e-4 b2=0.9999 eps=1e-4) 20000 iters  +0.35     38s     plateaus
  prox-AL Jacobi T=500 K=2 grow=0.995 mu=0.3          +0.07     2.3s    tied for LP-inner best
  prox-AL Gauss-Seidel T=200 K=2 mu=0.3               +0.09     1.5s    fastest LP-tight
  joint LP (Gurobi LP solver, _build_reference)       +0.00     0.03s   LP triangle exact

For LP-inner, all paths converge toward the LP triangle bound (-163.5993).
Joint LP via Gurobi reaches it in 0.03 s. Iterative methods take 1-4 s. All
of the iterative work on LP-inner was *strictly worse* than just calling
Gurobi on the joint LP relaxation.

1.3 MILP-inner (with big-M binary ReLU encoding per layer)
----------------------------------------------------------
At any fixed ρ, MILP-inner gives a tighter q(ρ) than LP-inner because
MILP-inner's per-sub feasible set is the exact non-convex ReLU set rather
than the triangle relaxation. The bound at the optimal LP-inner ρ becomes
substantially tighter once we evaluate it under MILP-inner subs.

  method                                              bound      wall
  -------------------------------------------------   --------   ------
  SGD MILP-inner cold start, 30 iters                 -118.66    125 s
  SGD MILP-inner cold start, 60 iters                 -120.99    128 s   (worse - noise)
  prox-AL Jacobi MILP-inner T=10 K=2 cold start       -102.17    179 s   prior best
  prox-AL Jacobi MILP-inner cold @ 200 s              -96.46     200 s
  warm-start LP 5s + MILP prox-AL 55s                 -95.73     60 s

Per-iter costs are dominated by the per-layer MILPs (50 binaries each).
Cold-start MILP runs hit the per-sub TimeLimit=5s repeatedly because the LP
relaxation at high-magnitude ρ is loose, the search tree explodes, and
ObjBound moves slowly.

1.4 The LP warm-start observation
---------------------------------
Running LP-inner LD to LP-tight (~2 s of SGD) and then doing ONE MILP-inner
solve at that ρ already beats every cold-start MILP-inner trace and reaches
a tighter bound than 30 s of joint Gurobi MILP:

  2 s LP-inner SGD -> ρ_LP, then 1 MILP-inner cycle
    before MILP iter (1.77 s):  best=-163.79  gap +0.19
    after  MILP iter (2.56 s):  best= -99.71  gap -63.89
  Delta from one MILP iter: +64.08 units of bound improvement.

This was the moment the picture shifted. Most of the gain was happening at
the LP -> MILP switch, *not* during the iterative dual updates of either
phase.

1.5 The strong-duality observation
----------------------------------
By LP strong duality applied to the LD reformulation:

  joint LP primal optimum  =  max over ρ of q_LP(ρ)  =  LP triangle bound

and the maximizer ρ_LP* is exactly the LP dual variables on the LD
"consistency" constraints z_P = z_C (when those constraints are made
explicit by giving each sub its own copy of the shared variables).

So instead of running 2 s of SGD to converge toward ρ_LP*, we can build the
LD-shaped joint LP in Gurobi (with explicit consistency constraints), solve
once, and read constraint.Pi as ρ. Total wall: 0.034 s.

After that, one MILP-inner cycle at ρ_LP* gives:
  joint LD-shaped LP: build 0.018 s + solve 0.016 s = 0.034 s
                      ObjVal = -163.5993 (matches LP triangle exactly)
  one MILP-inner solve at ρ:  0.636 s
  TOTAL                       0.735 s
  q_under_MILP                -95.27   (gap LP -68.33)

Sign convention note: Gurobi's constraint.Pi for "producer == consumer" is
the negative of our LD ρ convention (because Gurobi writes the constraint's
Lagrangian term as π·(b - Ax) while our set_obj uses +ρ·(producer-consumer)).
We auto-detect by running an LP-inner sanity check at both signs and picking
the one that reproduces the LP triangle bound.

1.6 Decomposition geometry exploration
--------------------------------------
With ρ extracted in 0.034 s, the only remaining variable is the per-sub
MILP cost. We tried three decomposition geometries:

  structure                       wall      bound       gap LP    notes
  ----------------------------   --------  ----------  --------  -----
  split G=2 (12 layer subs)       0.65 s    -122.67   -40.93    smaller MILPs, less correlation
  baseline (6 layer subs)         0.74 s     -95.27   -68.33    1 sub per layer
  merged 2-layer (3 subs)         7.04 s     -68.92   -94.68    100 binaries per sub

The split variant (each layer's 50 outputs into 2 groups of ~25) has smaller
MILPs but breaks within-layer cross-output correlations. Bound looser by 27
units, but slightly faster wall (because each MILP scales super-linearly in
binary count and the smaller MILPs more than make up for having twice as many
of them).

The merged variant ([[0,1], [2,3], [4,5]]) chains two layers' worth of binary
encoding into each sub, capturing exact ReLU correlations across consecutive
layers via shared joint variables. Bound tighter by 27 units, ~10x slower wall.

1.7 The mixed-relaxation experiment
-----------------------------------
The most striking result. We can independently choose LP triangle vs MILP big-M
per layer. Same LP-extracted ρ across all configurations, just swap the
per-sub relaxation choice:

  config                              q          gap LP     wall
  --------------------------------    ---------  --------   -----
  all LP                              -163.5993    +0.00    0.00 s
  only L0 = MILP                      -163.5973    -0.00    0.01 s
  only L1 = MILP                      -163.5972    -0.00    0.06 s
  only L2 = MILP                      -163.5368    -0.06    0.09 s
  only L3 = MILP                      -163.3261    -0.27    0.15 s
  only L4 = MILP                      -159.5487    -4.05    0.15 s
  only L5 = MILP                       -99.6641   -63.94    0.21 s   *
  L4,L5 = MILP                         -95.6135   -67.99    0.36 s
  L3,L4,L5 = MILP                      -95.3403   -68.26    0.50 s
  L2,L3,L4,L5 = MILP                   -95.2777   -68.32    0.59 s
  all MILP                             -95.2736   -68.33    0.64 s

MILP-encoding only the spec-adjacent layer (L5) delivers 94 % of the total
bound improvement at 33 % of the wall. Adding L4 buys 4 more units. After
that the contribution from L0..L3 is essentially noise.

The intuition: ρ on the spec-adjacent coupling is the largest in magnitude
(it directly carries the spec's qw vector through one weight matrix), so
that's where the LP triangle relaxation has the most slack to give. The
further you walk back from the spec, the more "diluted" the ρ values get
and the more the LP relaxation already nearly closes the gap.


2. THE FINAL ALGORITHM
======================

Stripped to its essentials, for spec lower bound computation on a sequential
ReLU network with phase-1 (l, u) bounds already in hand:

  Step 1.  Build LD-shaped joint LP:
             - one fresh copy of "input from layer k-1" per layer k
             - explicit consistency constraints producer == consumer linking
               consecutive subs
             - LP triangle relaxation throughout
             - objective is qw^T y + qb (the spec)
  Step 2.  Solve as a single LP (Gurobi dual simplex). Wall: ~0.02 s.
  Step 3.  Read constraint.Pi for every consistency constraint -> rho dict.
  Step 4.  Sign-detect: run LP-inner LD check at both ±rho, keep the sign
           that reproduces the LP solve's ObjVal.
  Step 5.  Pick a tightening set L_milp (subset of layers).
           Build per-layer subs with LP triangle for layers not in L_milp
           and big-M MILP for layers in L_milp.
  Step 6.  Set each sub's objective using rho. Solve all subs once.
  Step 7.  Best lower bound = qb + sum of per-sub objective values.

For ACAS Xu q0, choosing L_milp = {5} (the spec-adjacent layer) gives
gap LP -63.94 in 0.21 s. Choosing L_milp = {3,4,5} gives gap LP -68.26
in 0.50 s. Choosing L_milp = {0,1,2,3,4,5} (all layers) gives gap LP
-68.33 in 0.64 s.

Which subset to pick is a wall-vs-tightness knob. For sequential networks,
the heuristic that emerged from this study is: start with the spec-adjacent
layer and work backwards, stopping when the marginal gain from the next
layer becomes small relative to its MILP cost. On q0 the marginal-gain
profile is L5 = +63.94, L4 = +4.05, L3 = +0.27, L2 = +0.06, L1 = ε, L0 = ε,
so {5} or {4,5} captures essentially everything.

4. WHY THE BOUND IS VALID
=========================

The headline result -- "extract ρ from joint LP duals, swap one sub to MILP,
sum, get a tighter bound than the LP triangle relaxation in 0.21 seconds" --
deserves careful justification because it mixes LP duality with non-convex
(MILP) per-sub solves. Three claims to defend:

  CLAIM A. q(ρ) := Σ_subs min(sub_obj at ρ) is a valid lower bound on the
           original spec problem, for ANY ρ, regardless of whether the per-sub
           min is computed over LP-relaxed or MILP feasible sets.

  CLAIM B. The ρ extracted from constraint.Pi of the LD-shaped joint LP
           equals the optimal LP-Lagrangian-dual ρ (the maximizer of q_LP).

  CLAIM C. Switching one or more sub from LP-relaxed to MILP-exact at fixed
           ρ produces a bound that is monotonically (weakly) tighter than
           the all-LP bound at the same ρ. The resulting bound is still
           valid, by weak Lagrangian duality.

Defense:

A. WEAK LAGRANGIAN DUALITY (the only thing the validity rests on)

The original spec problem is

    min  qw^T y(x) + qb
    s.t. x in X (input box)
         all internal network constraints
         all ReLU constraints (exact, with binary indicators)

Equivalently, after introducing a private copy z_A,k of each producer's
post_relu z_B,k-1 and adding the consistency constraint z_A,k = z_B,k-1, the
problem decomposes into per-sub problems coupled only by those equality
constraints. Form the Lagrangian by relaxing the consistency constraints
with multipliers ρ (one entry per shared variable):

    L(x, ρ) = sum_subs base_obj_sub(x_sub) + ρ^T (z_B - z_A)
    q(ρ)    = min over feasible-per-sub-x of L(x, ρ)
            = sum_subs [ min over feasible_sub_set of (base_obj_sub + ρ-coupled terms) ]

Weak Lagrangian duality (a basic textbook result, holds with NO assumptions
on convexity):

    q(ρ) <= original spec optimum, for every ρ.

The "feasible_sub_set" in the inner min can be ANY subset of the original
sub feasible set without breaking the inequality, as long as it stays a
subset (which is always the case because MILP-exact ⊂ LP-triangle-relaxed).
Specifically:

    min over MILP-exact ≥ min over LP-triangle (because MILP set is smaller).

But the dual inequality goes the other way:

    q_MILP(ρ) := sum_subs [min over MILP-exact (...)]
               ≥ q_LP(ρ) := sum_subs [min over LP-relaxed (...)]

and BOTH satisfy the dual inequality, because the original problem is
constrained by the (smaller) MILP-exact feasible set. Weak duality:

    q_MILP(ρ) <= original_optimum     (for any ρ)
    q_LP(ρ)   <= original_optimum     (for any ρ)

So q evaluated under MILP-inner subs at any ρ is a valid lower bound on the
original. This is Claim A. The validity does not depend on:
  - convexity of the inner sets (we explicitly use non-convex MILP)
  - the warm-started primal x^k being feasible (the bound never reads x^k;
    it only reads the per-sub objective values at the optimum of each sub)
  - the iteration converging (any single evaluation of q at any ρ is valid)
  - ρ being optimal (any ρ works, optimal ρ just gives a tighter bound)

This is the fundamental safety net.

B. LP STRONG DUALITY GIVES THE OPTIMAL LP-LAGRANGIAN ρ FOR FREE

For the LP-relaxed version of the problem, all constraints are linear and
the feasible set is convex. Strong LP duality applies. The LP-Lagrangian
relaxation of the LD-decomposed LP is:

    max over ρ of q_LP(ρ)  =  LP triangle bound  =  joint LP primal optimum

The LD-shaped joint LP we build (with explicit consistency constraints,
each sub having its own copy of the shared variables) has:
  - LP primal optimum equal to the LP triangle bound (because the
    decomposition with explicit equality constraints is mathematically
    equivalent to the un-split LP -- the constraints just say "two copies
    of the same quantity are equal").
  - LP dual optimum equal to LP primal optimum, by LP strong duality.
  - LP dual variable for each consistency constraint = the ρ value that
    achieves the LP-Lagrangian dual optimum on that coupling.

Therefore, calling Gurobi on the LD-shaped joint LP and reading
constraint.Pi yields the optimal LP-Lagrangian ρ. The 2 seconds of SGD or
prox-AL we ran earlier was iterating toward exactly this ρ. Gurobi gets it
in 16 ms by solving the LP exactly. This is Claim B.

(The sign-detection step exists only because Gurobi's convention for the
Lagrangian sign of an equality constraint is the negative of ours; the
magnitudes are unaffected.)

C. SWAPPING IN MILP SUBS AT FIXED ρ TIGHTENS THE BOUND

At the LP-extracted ρ_LP*, we have:

    q_LP(ρ_LP*) = LP triangle bound (-163.5993 on q0)

For each sub i, the LP-relaxed sub minimum equals some value v_LP,i, and
the MILP-exact sub minimum equals some value v_MILP,i ≥ v_LP,i (since the
MILP feasible set is a subset of the LP feasible set).

If we swap sub i from LP to MILP and leave the others alone, the new
aggregated bound is:

    q_mixed(ρ_LP*)  =  Σ_{j != i} v_LP,j  +  v_MILP,i
                    ≥ Σ_{j != i} v_LP,j  +  v_LP,i
                    =  q_LP(ρ_LP*)
                    =  LP triangle bound.

So the mixed bound is at least as tight as the LP triangle bound. By weak
Lagrangian duality (Claim A), it is also a valid lower bound on the
original spec optimum:

    q_mixed(ρ_LP*) ≤ original spec optimum.

Combining: LP_triangle ≤ q_mixed ≤ original_optimum. Tighter than the LP
triangle, valid as a spec lower bound. This is Claim C.

The "swap to MILP" operation can be applied to any subset of subs
independently. The bound improvement is monotone: adding more layers to
the MILP set (while keeping ρ fixed) can only tighten or leave unchanged
each individual sub's contribution, so the sum can only tighten or stay
the same.

ρ_LP* is NOT the optimal MILP-Lagrangian dual (which would maximize
q_MILP). The true optimal ρ for q_MILP would give an even tighter bound.
Iterating dual ascent on top of the mixed evaluation (e.g., the prox-AL
runs we tried earlier) is one way to chase that optimum. On q0, the
empirical observation was that the additional iterative work bought only
a few units beyond what the one-shot LP-dual + selective MILP gave us.

D. WHAT IS NOT CLAIMED

The bound is valid; it is also strict (not necessarily tight to the true
spec optimum). Specifically:

  - q_mixed(ρ_LP*) is generally a strict under-estimate of the true spec
    minimum because (i) Lagrangian relaxation has a duality gap on
    non-convex problems, and (ii) we're not at the optimal MILP-Lagrangian ρ.
  - The bound depends on the per-layer (l, u) bounds from phase 1. If
    those are loose, the big-M's are loose and the per-sub MILPs are
    correspondingly looser.
  - The bound is per-query. To verify a multi-query disjunct, you need
    every query's bound to be ≥ 0 (or to satisfy the disjunct logic).

The validity argument carries over without modification to:
  - networks with skip connections / merge-Add (each merge becomes a
    consistency constraint with multiple producer terms)
  - mixed convolution + FC layers
  - the "merged groups" and "split groups" decomposition geometries
    (the per-sub feasible sets just change shape; weak duality still holds)


SUMMARY IN ONE PARAGRAPH
========================

For a sequential ReLU network with phase-1 (l, u) bounds, build the joint
LP relaxation in LD-decomposed form (each layer with its own copy of
inputs, explicit producer == consumer equality constraints). Solve as a
single LP. The constraint duals are the optimal LP-Lagrangian multipliers
ρ_LP*. Plug ρ_LP* into per-layer subs where each sub independently uses
either LP triangle or big-M MILP encoding for its ReLUs. Solve each sub
once. Sum the per-sub objective values; add qb. The result is a valid
lower bound on the spec, strictly tighter than the LP triangle bound, and
the tightness vs wall trade-off is controlled by which subset of layers
get the MILP treatment. For the ACAS Xu 3_3 prop_2 q0 instance, MILPing
just the spec-adjacent layer takes 0.21 s and gives a bound (-99.66) that
is tighter than 30 seconds of joint Gurobi spec-MILP and tighter than
200 seconds of prox-AL on cold-start MILP-inner.
